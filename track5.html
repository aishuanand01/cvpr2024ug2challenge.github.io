Track 5: UAV Tracking and Pose-estimation

Register for this track

Recently, UAVs have become a major concern in frontline combat, contraband smuggling, and the endangerment of commercial aircraft, among other areas. Due to their compact size and quiet electric motors, they are difficult to detect by soldiers, law-enforcement and vehicles.

This challenge aims to address the evolving challenge posed by small unmanned aerial vehicles in imagery and beyond based on a comprehensive Multi-Modal Anti-UAV Dataset (MMAUD). This challenge fills a critical gap in contemporary threat detection methodologies by focusing on drone detection, UAV-type classification, and trajectory estimation. What sets this challenge apart is its integration of diverse sensory inputs, including stereo vision, various LiDARs, radars, and audio arrays.

The newly introduced dataset in this challenge offers a unique overhead aerial perspective crucial for addressing real-world scenarios with greater fidelity than datasets or challenges captured from specific vantage points using thermal and RGB cameras. Additionally, this MMAUD dataset provides precise ground truth data generated by a survey-graded Leica total station, enhancing its credibility and enabling the confident refinement of algorithms and models—a feature unseen in other datasets or challenges. Our challenge closely simulates real-world scenarios by incorporating ambient sounds from heavy machinery. This approach enhances the solution's applicability, capturing the exact challenges faced during proximity to vehicular operations. This challenge is expected to play a pivotal role in advancing UAV threat detection, classification, trajectory estimation capabilities, and more.

Participants' solutions will be evaluated in terms of UAV type classification accuracy, 3D trajectory estimation accuracy, cost, and processing time. This evaluation comes from a real need for solutions that are affordable, quick, and accurate, which are highly desired. On the other hand, solutions that involve more sensors, are slow, or prone to errors are not wanted. Participants need to select balanced algorithms from the given options to successfully classify the UAV and estimate its trajectory.

In the challenge, every participant is required to submit a fact sheet that outlines their methods, data modality used, runtime, model size, and hardware requirements, among other details. After the challenge, all fact sheets will be gathered into a final report to spotlight emerging trends and innovative techniques. Additionally, participants are invited to submit detailed manuscripts about their methods to the workshop.
